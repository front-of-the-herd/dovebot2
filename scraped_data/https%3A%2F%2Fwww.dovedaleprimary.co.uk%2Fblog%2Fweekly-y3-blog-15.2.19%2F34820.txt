Weekly Y3 Blog 15.2.19
Date:
15th Feb 2019 @ 1:24pm
This week in Year 3, we have been working extremely hard and enjoying the following activities:
Maths – A range of money problems involving:
Converting pounds and pence
Using money in practical contexts
English – We have focused on cross-curricular History writing during our English lessons this week. We have written a diary entry from the perspective of a Bronze Age child who has to go down the mine for the very first time.
History – We have focused the changes from the Stone Age to the Bronze Age
Science – Plants – We looked at our investigation we set up several weeks ago to find out if a plant needs light and soil to survive and grow.
P.E – Dance
Music, PSHE and French
After half term in Year 3, we shall be enjoying the following activities:
Maths - We shall continue our work on money for one more week. During this unit, we will focus on the following objectives:
Adding money
Subtracting money
Giving change
Using money in practical contexts
English – We shall start our explanation topic using the story of Lego. Over half term, watch https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NdDU_BBJW9Y
Geography – Where on Earth are we? Over this unit, we shall focus on the following objectives:
Improve locational knowledge through identifying the position and significance of latitude, longitude, Equator, Northern Hemisphere, South Hemisphere, the Tropics of Cancer and Capricorn, Arctic and Antarctic Circle
Practice geographical skills through using maps, atlases, globes and digital/computer mapping to locate features
Use the eight points of the compass to build their knowledge of the wider world
Science – Light
Music, Computing and French
